# Data Management

Data management is the process of organizing and attaching meaning to data so that it can be used effectively by businesses and computer programs. Data is inert — not self-organized or self-understandable — and requires structured approaches to realize value.

## The DIKW Pyramid

The DIKW pyramid visualizes the value chain from raw data to wisdom:

- **Data** — The base, with the least perceived usefulness. Inert and unstructured.
- **Information** — Data with added understanding through programs, machines, or humans.
- **Knowledge** — Organized and classified information with higher value.
- **Wisdom** — The highest perceived value, combining data with perspective, context, understanding, learning, and reasoning.

## Data Progression Phases

AI/ML projects follow four phases to transform data into actionable capability:

1. **Collect** — Making data simple and accessible. Attaching to data sources regardless of their form (structured, semi-structured, unstructured) or location (cloud, on-premise). Data at this stage is inert, lying below the first rung of the DIKW pyramid.

2. **Organize** — Trusted, governed analytics. Making data discoverable, cataloged, profiled, categorized, classified, and secured through policy-based enforcement. The outcome is a curated body of trusted data.

3. **Analyze** — Insights on-demand. Becoming data-driven through analytics ranging from basic reporting to deep learning. AI can be interpreted as Augmented Intelligence (human-centered) or Artificial Intelligence (machine-centered). Enables determining what has happened, what is happening, what might happen, and automating decisions.

4. **Infuse** — Operationalize AI with trust and transparency. Embedding AI as real-world capability into the essential fabric of applications. Requires model management, transparency (explainable outcomes), and trust (fairness). Creates causal loops of action and feedback within data-centric enterprises.
