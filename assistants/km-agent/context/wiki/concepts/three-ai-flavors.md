# Three AI Flavors

With the deployment of Generative AI, the term "AI" needs more specificity. Three distinct flavors of AI are useful for addressing business problems:

## Symbolic AI
Expert System AI and knowledge graphs used to represent human knowledge with rules and semantic relationships. This approach codes decisions with inference rules to express domain and expert knowledge. Recommended reading includes *Agile Business Rules Development* by Hafedh Mili and J. Boyer.

## Analytical AI
Machine-learned models from data using analytical algorithms to solve tasks such as classification, clustering, predictive scoring, or evaluation. Early neural networks were used to support better classification on unstructured data like images and text. ML helps when developers do not have knowledge of all rule conditions, and decision trees can be discovered from data.

## Generative AI
Generates new content (text, image, audio) from existing large corpora of unstructured data. It uses deep learning, NLP, the transformer architecture, image recognition, and voice recognition. Most Generative AI and LLMs are deployed as SaaS with API access.

All three flavors are complementary and useful depending on the business problem being addressed.
